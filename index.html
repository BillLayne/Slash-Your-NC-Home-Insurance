5. Why does my credit score affect my home insurance rate?

Your credit score affects your home insurance rate because insurance companies use a credit-based "insurance score" to predict the likelihood you'll file a claim. Statistically, individuals with higher credit scores file fewer claims, so insurers reward them with lower premiums. This factor can significantly impact your rates in North Carolina.

It might not seem fair, but it's a reality of modern insurance. The good news is that different companies weigh this "insurance score" differently. If you have a less-than-perfect credit history, some national insurers might hit you with outrageous rates.
